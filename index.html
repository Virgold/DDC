Texas 6-Hour Defensive Driving Course

Eligibility

This course is for drivers who are improving their defensive driving knowledge and skills. For someone learning to get better at driving on the road, this course is just for you. However, we have a few details to consider.

You should have the following to be eligible for the course:

  • Valid non-commercial Texas driver license or permit
  • You did not take this course or any similar program to dismiss a traffic ticket within the past 12 months.
  • Signed a statement or admitted guilt to the violation, and that you are not contesting the ticket.

Contact your court to be certain that your ticket can get dismissed. While there a plenty of tickets that this course can address, some violations may not be expelled from your license at all. Examples of which are severe offenses involving injury or property damage, felony, passing a school bus, hit-and-run, or DUI.

Objectives of the Course

The primary objective of the 6-hour defensive driving course is to provide drivers with knowledge on the rules of the road, traffic safety, and defensive driving techniques. It aims to instill responsible driving behavior, increase awareness of potential risks on the road, and promote safe driving habits.
By the end of this course, you will:

  • Develop a thorough understanding of traffic laws and regulations specific to your region;
  • Familiarize yourself with the meaning and importance of various traffic signs, signals, and road markings;
  • Gain awareness of potential hazards and develop the skills necessary to recognize and react to them appropriately;
  • Learn defensive driving techniques to anticipate and avoid potential accidents and dangerous situations;
  • Understand the importance of maintaining a focused and attentive mindset while driving, and
  • Cultivate responsible driving behaviors and attitudes to promote road safety.

Rules and Regulations: How Does This Course Work?

For you to pass this course, it is essential to understand and adhere to the rules and regulations. Failure to comply with these requirements may result in being unable to pass the course and potentially being dismissed from the class. The following are the laws and rules:

  • Each course purchase includes a single access only—you—and no other person is permitted to access the course in any way, not even to log in.
  • The institute is the exclusive owner of the course's intellectual property, which is protected by copyright regulations. Without consent, this should not be disclosed to the public. If such behavior is discovered, the offender will be immediately dismissed from the class, and appropriate legal action will be taken.
  • The amount of time spent on the course is tracked. According to Texas law, you must spend at least 6 HOURS on the course for it to be recognized as successful. You must log in to the course using your login credential to track your time.
  • At the conclusion of the course, you will be required to pass a final qualifying exam to complete it. To pass the course, you must achieve a minimum accuracy of 70%.
